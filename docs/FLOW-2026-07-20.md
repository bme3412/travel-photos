# Product flow map — 2026-07-20

Point-in-time walkthrough of every screen and how they connect, written the
day the destination-first routing landed. The living direction is
`ROADMAP.md`; this is what actually exists today.

## 1. Landing (`/`)

Statement headline + four destination cards (Berlin, Kraków, Menton,
Paris). Each card is a single door to `/destinations/<city>`. Below the
fold: the "How it works" band, the album archive grid (all albums,
including pre-2026 country trips — gallery links with direct Replay
buttons), and the dispatch feed. A resume banner appears when a copy
session exists in localStorage and deep-links to wherever the visitor
left off.

Nav: Destinations · Original Trips · Travel Map · Photo of the Day.

## 2. Destination index and hub

- `/destinations` — every city-scoped destination as a card (cover, visit
  years, photograph count, available-to-copy chip).
- `/destinations/<city>` — the decision point. Two front-door actions:
  **Replay the trip** (newest visit's replay) and **Copy this trip**
  (rendered only when a blueprint exists). Below: one card per visit with
  its three lenses (Replay · Story · Photos), then the neighborhoods grid.
  The page deepens automatically when a new `<city>-<year>` visit lands.

## 3. The evidence layer (visit-scoped, keyed by `<city>-<year>`)

- `/trips/<id>` — the flagship scene replay: a scrollytelling day film
  from `narratives.json` `report`, GPS-anchored photos on the map. Every
  experience has "+ Add to my trip" (seeds the copy session in place);
  the view switcher carries a persistent Copy CTA; "Every visit to
  {city} →" links up to the hub.
- `/journal/<id>` and `/albums/<id>` — story and photographs; all three
  lenses cross-link and preserve day/scene focus.
- `/trips` — index of all replays, including legacy country albums that
  have no destination page.

## 4. The copy flow (`/destinations/<city>/copy/*`)

URLs are destination-keyed; sessions and blueprints stay keyed by the
visit's trip id (`getCopySourceTripId` resolves slug → newest blueprint;
`copyFlowHref` builds every copy URL).

1. **Overview** (`/copy`) — the source-trip pitch: route preview, one
   frame per day, dates/pace/party facts. (Kept as a deliberate step,
   owner decision 2026-07-20.)
2. **Select** (`/copy/select`) — keep/remove/must-keep days and
   experiences, plus guide additions from the neighborhood registry's
   `copyOptions`.
3. **Personalize** (`/copy/personalize`) — dates, party, pace, budget,
   transformation preferences; feasibility warning when the kept
   experiences don't fit the requested days at the requested pace.
4. **Result** (`/copy/result`) — calls `/api/copy-trip/generate`
   (Claude + deterministic rules + `crossCheckPlan` provenance audit),
   renders the personalized itinerary with the comparison module and the
   provenance drawer tracing each stop to its source moment.

## 5. State

The whole session lives in localStorage (Zustand persist,
`copy-trip-session`): selections, preferences, generated plan, saved
copies. Survives reloads; nothing server-side. Generate API is
rate-limited per IP and per instance.

## 6. Quiet layers

`/neighborhoods` (registry, deliberately not in the nav), `/map`,
`/photo-of-the-day`, `/studio` (dev-only narrative editor). Old
`/cities/*` and `/trips/<id>/copy/*` URLs 301 to `/destinations/*`.

## Known weak spots (as of this date)

- **The result screen is a dead end** — no share/export. Flagged in the
  roadmap as the feature's payoff.
- **Two products share one house**: the destination product (4 cities,
  copy-enabled) and the photo archive (everything else). The landing page
  below the fold is mostly archive; that seam is where confusion tends to
  live.
