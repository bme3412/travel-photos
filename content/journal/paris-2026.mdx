Three days in Paris in the middle of June — a single city held close rather than a
country crossed. The itinerary was simple: the Tower, the table, and the streets
in between. That focus was the point; one place, given room to unfold at its own
pace.

<Figure src="IMG_0088.jpg" caption="Three days given wholly to one city." />

## The Tower

The Eiffel Tower sat at the center of the days — that familiar iron silhouette
returned to across the hours, caught from different streets and different angles.
Some landmarks disappoint up close; this one keeps its scale, drawing the eye back
again and again from across the city.

## The table and the streets

Between the landmarks came the ordinary pleasures — the local cuisine, the meals
that anchor a day in Paris as much as any monument, and the long walks between
them. No onward distance to cover, no next stop pulling ahead; just the unhurried
business of being there.

<Gallery srcs={["IMG_0128.jpg", "IMG_0134.jpg", "IMG_0139.jpg", "IMG_0145.jpg", "IMG_0150.jpg", "IMG_0156.jpg"]} caption="The table, and the streets in between." />

Three days, one city, walked slowly and lingered over.
