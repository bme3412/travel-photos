Paris every June, alone, timed to the Fête de la Musique — the same ritual, a
city I already half-know by heart. This year it came in a heatwave: three days
brushing 99°F, the kind of heat that empties the famous staircases and sends
everyone into the shade. From an apartment on Rue Dauphine, a few minutes from
Pont Neuf, the whole weekend unspooled on foot and by métro.

<Figure src="IMG_0077.jpg" caption="Off the plane and down to the river — the first afternoon near Pont Neuf." />

## The neighborhoods

Base was Saint-Germain-des-Prés: Café de Flore in the morning, the old streets I
studied among back in 2009, coffees that stretched into afternoons. The first day
climbed north to Montmartre and up to Sacré-Cœur, where the heat had done
something strange — the great staircase, usually elbow to elbow, sat almost empty.
The pink house and the little vineyard, the study-abroad quarter out by Gare de
l'Est: a Paris of memory as much as sightseeing.

## The heart of the city

Most of the weekend turned around the two islands. Pont Neuf and the green tip of
the Square du Vert-Galant, Notre-Dame across the water, and Île Saint-Louis —
where lunch was moules-frites and a street jazz band, and dessert was Berthillon.
The Marais just beyond, all narrow lanes and squares. This is the Paris I keep
coming back for: dense, walkable, endlessly turned over.

## The Tower

You can't not. The Eiffel Tower held the middle of the trip — seen across the
river by day, and again at night from a Bateau Mouche gliding the Seine, the iron
lit up over the black water. Some landmarks shrink up close; this one keeps its
scale, pulling the eye back from every bridge and quay.

<Gallery srcs={["IMG_0135.jpg", "IMG_0136.jpg", "IMG_0137.jpg", "IMG_0138.jpg", "IMG_0123.jpg"]} caption="The Eiffel Tower, by day and lit from the river at night." />

## The grand axis

And the monumental spine of the city — the gilded dome of Les Invalides, the
Tuileries and the Louvre, and, on the last morning, up the Champs-Élysées to the
Arc de Triomphe, paid for and climbed for the long straight view down the
avenues. A hot Sunday of the Fête, the city loud with music, before the walk back
and the flight out.

Three days, one city, walked to the edge of the heat — and already, the same
weekend booked for next year.
